Lesson 7

Sentences

leeiq sha zhan qoum kka sha gy dy1, 2, 3 I like the person who is next to me
zeai na sym4 What is this?
leio ta hy len zaai sym Where are you sitting?
neou sha giau maauq gy dy5 I know that mountains are big
leuiq zhan hoo kka maq gy sha dy The person that eats food is next to me
  1. When the first vowel of a word is o, it is a sentence that is describing a noun. For example, the phrase “the person who drinks water”. “Drink water” is what the person does.
  2. When a sentence is describing a noun, the word kka refers to the noun that is being described. In “the person who drinks water”, “who” is kka in Laiqbun, but as you learnt in the first lesson, verbs always go first in Laiqbun. For now, lets pretend kka has no form other than its noun form.
  3. When a sentence is describing a noun, and you wish to go back to the main part of the sentence, you use gy.
  4. The verb zeai means “x1 is what?” and is used for questions that are not yes/no.
  5. When the first vowel of a word is i, it is a sentence that behaves as a noun. For example, in the sentence “I know things”, “things” is a noun. Now, in a sentence like “I know that you did this”, “you did this” takes the same place as “things”, even though it has a verb. Similar to how it is with o words, if you wish to end an i word and return to the main sentence, you use gy.

Translate

zeai zhan hoo kka tauo gy sym? The person that drinks water is who?
len kaao kkeuacuan tam lon kka maauq gy dy The blue bird is in the house on the mountain
neou sha kkiuacuan kaao ne gy dy I know that this bird is blue
heo maao pou zhan zeai kka gy tauo sym? Whose cat is drinking water?
